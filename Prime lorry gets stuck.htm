Amazon lorry's tight squeeze in Cornwall delivery attempt

Olivier Vergnault
~2 minutes

A huge articulated lorry once again proved how you can never fully rely on sat navs when travelling along the narrow roads of Cornwall after ending up in a tight squeeze in a seaside village. The unfortunate Amazon lorry driver found himself in a tight spot when he travelled to St Agnes on the North Coast and almost got wedged between two Grade-II listed buildings.

The 33-tonne lorry in the instantly recognisable Amazon livery had travelled down the main road into the village before reaching the tricky point between the Churchtown Arts store and the Grove Cottage and Stippy Stappy on Town Hill.

Residents in St Agnes said the incident happened shortly before 5pm on Friday, August 18. One local said he spoke to the driver who told him he'd followed and trusted the sat nav.

He said: "He wouldn't reverse and kept driving forward. After about 25 minutes and multiple dents on the vehicle he managed to free the lorry. I hope he hasn't damaged the grade-II listed buildings in the village."

The bemused resident said this kind of sat nav fail has happened in St Agnes in the past but never to that extent. He joked: "The lovely driver blamed Satnav. Don't Amazon sell proper Satnavs for HGVs?"
